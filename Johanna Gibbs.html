Sexy Woman, Sad Woman

Johanna Gibbs


'Sexy Woman, Sad Woman: an Inventory of the Primary Features of the Visual Language of Ten Tabloid Magazines from the Last Week of February/First Week of March 2022’ looks at the univernacular language of tabloid magazines across the world. While everything was falling apart, the tabloid magazine industry kept kicking — presenting light and salacious stories that allow for their scandal to be interpreted without language but instead through accepted cliches. My 352 page book, kettle bound, breaks these strategies down into 16 main categories, with further splits according to how the content of elements such as cropping, degree of angle and number of lines of text. Throughout the process of putting together this book, what became clear was that in tabloid design, the image reigns supreme. All choices champion the hero and supplementary images on the page, which makes the tabloid both a great example of the univernacular and an interesting study of smaller graphic differences across the world.
